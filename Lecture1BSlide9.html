From Leibniz's Theorem to the Reynolds Transport Theorem

Starting with:

\[ \frac{d}{dt} \int_{V^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{1}{\Delta t} \left\{ \int_{V^*(t)} \Delta t \frac{\partial F(\mathbf{x},t)}{\partial t} dV + \int_{\Delta V} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV \right\} \]

Substituting Equation:

\[ \int_{\Delta V} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV \approx \int_{A^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) (\mathbf{b} \Delta t \cdot \mathbf{n}) dA. \]

Substituting:

\[ \frac{d}{dt} \int_{V^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{1}{\Delta t} \left\{ \int_{V^*(t)} \Delta t \frac{\partial F(\mathbf{x},t)}{\partial t} dV + \fbox{$\int_{\Delta V} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV$} \right\} \]

Replacing the boxed term:

\[ \frac{d}{dt} \int_{V^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{1}{\Delta t} \left\{ \int_{V^*(t)} \Delta t \frac{\partial F(\mathbf{x},t)}{\partial t} dV + \fbox{$\int_{A^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) (\mathbf{b} \Delta t \cdot \mathbf{n}) dA$} \right\} \]

\( \Delta t \) is a common factor, divide by \( \Delta t \):

\[ \frac{d}{dt} \int\limits_{V^*(t)} F(\mathbf{x},t) dV = \int\limits_{\substack{V^*(t)}} \frac{\partial F(\mathbf{x},t)}{\partial t} dV + \int\limits_{\substack{A^*(t)}} F(\mathbf{x},t) \mathbf{b} \cdot \mathbf{n} dA. \]

It produces the statement of Reynolds Transport Theorem.
