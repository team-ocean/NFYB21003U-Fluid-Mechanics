Problem 6: Properties of Fluids

(1) The dimensionless Mach number is defined as \[ \text{Ma} = \frac{V}{c} = \frac{\text{Speed of flow}}{\text{Speed of sound}} \] where $c$ is the speed of sound, whose value is 346 m/s in air at room temperature at sea level. A flow is called sonic when Ma = 1, subsonic when Ma < 1, supersonic when Ma > 1, and hypersonic when Ma ≫ 1.
